About me

My name is Danilo Ferreira da Rocha, and I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at the Laboratório Nacional de Astrofísica (LNA), Brazil. I work in observational stellar astrophysics, with a focus on massive stars, particularly Be stars and hierarchical multiple systems, using photometric, spectroscopic, and interferometric data in the optical and near-infrared. I hold a PhD in Astronomy from the Observatório Nacional (2025), including a research internship at the Observatoire de Paris (2024), as well as an MSc in Physics. Alongside research, I am actively involved in science outreach and education, mainly through the Brazilian Astronomy and Astronautics Olympiad (OBA), and I served as a jury member of the IOAA 2024. Outside academia, I enjoy listening to music (MPB and Brazilian rock), going to the beach, and playing electronic games.

Research

  • Massive Stars

    Stars with initial masses ≳ 8 M☉, highly luminous, rapidly evolving, and with a strong impact on the evolution of galaxies.

  • Binary/Multiple Systems

    Binary/Multiple systems are gravitationally bound stellar systems composed of two or more stars, often arranged in a hierarchical configuration.

  • Be Stars

    Be stars are rapidly rotating B-type stars that exhibit, or have exhibited, Balmer emission lines due to a circumstellar gaseous disk.
